## 1. Health Landscape of University Students in Rwanda
Recent studies highlight a significant health burden among university students in Rwanda, particularly regarding mental health and infectious diseases.

* Mental Health Prevalence: Research conducted at the University of Rwanda found that nearly 38.5% of students exhibited symptoms of depression, while over 40% reported anxiety. These issues are often linked to academic pressure, financial stress, and social isolation.
* Barriers to Care: Students in Rwanda often face "information asymmetry," where they lack accurate knowledge about where to seek care or how to use their insurance, such as RSSB or Radiant. This lack of guidance can lead to delayed treatment and worsened health outcomes. [3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8] 
* 

## 2. AI and Machine Learning in Healthcare Triage
Artificial Intelligence (AI) has been increasingly adopted to solve triage challenges, where the goal is to prioritize patients based on severity. [9] 

* Efficiency in Triage: Systematic reviews show that AI and Machine Learning (ML) models significantly outperform conventional triage tools in predicting hospital admissions and mortality.
* Voice-Based Systems: Voice-AI systems have been shown to achieve 19% faster documentation compared to manual methods, making them ideal for high-stress or emergency situations where typing is difficult.
* Accuracy: LLMs like GPT-4 and specialized medical models have demonstrated high diagnostic accuracy, with some models reaching 88% accuracy in disease-level diagnosis. [9, 10, 11, 12] 
* 

## 3. On-Device LLMs and Privacy
Running Large Language Models (LLMs) directly on mobile devices is a growing trend that addresses the unique needs of healthcare.

* Privacy and Security: On-device deployment keeps sensitive health data local, eliminating the need for cloud-based services and significantly enhancing patient privacy.
* Performance: Compact models like Phi-3 Mini have been benchmarked to show a strong balance between speed and accuracy on mobile hardware, making them feasible for real-time clinical reasoning on the "edge". [13, 14] 
* 

## 4. Reinforcement Learning for Alignment (RLHF)
To ensure that an LLM provides safe and useful medical advice, Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) is essential.

* Safety Alignment: RLHF pipelines integrate human expertise (clinicians or first-aiders) to align model outputs with medical best practices and human values.
* Personalization: RL methods allow for personalized adaptive health interventions by incorporating user preferences and constraints into the model's policy, ensuring the advice is relevant to the specific context of the user—in this case, an ALU student in Kigali. [15, 16] 

## 5. Identified Gaps
While AI triage tools exist, there is a clear lack of localized, on-device mobile applications specifically designed for university students in the East African context. Most existing tools require a constant internet connection and do not account for local health insurance systems (like RSSB). Your project addresses this gap by combining on-device privacy with RL-tuned local medical knowledge. [9]
