# Rwanda Medical Insurance — Financial Policies

Summary of **financial** rules from:
- Britam Inpatient Medical Insurance Contract (corporate)
- Old Mutual Insurance Rwanda Medical Insurance Tariff

All amounts are in **Rwandan Francs (RWF)** unless noted.

---

## 1. Britam — Premium & Payment

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Policy type | **Inpatient only** (12-month term) |
| When premium is due | **Full annual premium before cover starts** |
| Cover start | After proposal accepted **and** premium paid in full |
| Currency | RWF |
| Premium invoice (renewal) | Sent **60 days** before renewal; reminder **30 days** before expiry |

**Premium bill components (corporate template):**

| Line item | Notes |
|-----------|--------|
| Basic inpatient premium | Main premium |
| Mutuelle de santé | **5%** of basic premium |
| Medical cards | One-time / per-card printing cost |
| **Grand total** | Sum of above |

**Late / unpaid premium:**

| Situation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Not paid by due date | Britam may **terminate policy without notice**; no benefits for unpaid periods |
| Paid within **30 days** after due date | Cover restored; no new application forms required |
| Outstanding **> 30 days** | Reinstatement needs health declarations; Britam may decline members |
| Outstanding **> 90 days** | Must **reapply** for the policy |
| Mid-year new members | Premium charged **pro-rata** for remaining period |

**Other premium rules:**

- Premium rates may change at renewal (loss ratio, member count, age, benefits).
- Members with higher claim risk may get loadings, exclusions, or special conditions.
- Card reprint (lost/stolen): cost borne by **member or employer**.
- Damaged card: member surrenders old card; printing cost borne by **member**.

## 2. Britam — Coverage Limits & Cost-Sharing

**Contract category referenced:** `RWANDA COVER — 100% NO CO-PAY`

| Financial concept | Policy |
|-------------------|--------|
| Co-payment | Defined as member share of **consultation fees** — but this schedule is **0% co-pay** |
| Annual caps | Set per **Schedule of Benefits** (contract template leaves amounts blank) |
| Who pays over limit | **Member / employer** pays anything above annual or sub-limits |
| Charge basis | **Reasonable and Customary Charges** (MINISANTE guidelines, provider agreements, Britam experience) |

**Benefit buckets (each has its own sub-limit under inpatient overall):**

| Benefit | Limit basis |
|---------|-------------|
| Inpatient overall | Per **family** |
| Maternity (inpatient) | Per **female spouse** |
| Congenital | Per family (within inpatient) |
| Non-accidental dental (inpatient) | Per family |
| Non-accidental ophthalmology (inpatient) | Per family |
| Pandemic management | Per family |
| Pre-existing, chronic, HIV/AIDS, psychiatric | Per family (declared chronic only) |
| Funeral / last expense | Per **death** |

**What Britam generally pays (inpatient):**

- Hospital room (standard private ward; excludes premium wings at named hospitals)
- Theatre, ICU, drugs in hospital, scans (CT/PET/MRI need approval)
- Day surgery under general anaesthesia (pre-authorised, 48h notice)
- Emergency road ambulance (life-threatening only)
- Maternity complications within maternity sub-limit
- Heart & kidney transplants (recipient only; donor/search costs excluded)

**What Britam does not pay (financially relevant):**

- Pre-existing conditions unless **declared and accepted in writing**
- **All outpatient** treatment
- Elective treatment without **prior written approval**
- Treatment outside approved benefits or above sub-limits
- Non-registered dependants
- Flight tickets (evacuation / overseas elective — treatment/accommodation only within limits)
- Travel & accommodation unless pre-approved in writing
- Experimental treatment, cosmetic surgery (except defined exceptions)
- Provider charges above reasonable & customary (member may owe difference)

**Network vs out-of-network:**

| Provider type | Payment |
|---------------|---------|
| Associated (in-network) | Direct settlement where agreed |
| Non-associated | Member pays upfront; reimbursed at **reasonable & customary** per schedule |

## 3. Britam — Claims & Reimbursement

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Claim deadline | **90 days** from date of treatment |
| Documentation | Original invoices/receipts + signed claim form (photocopies not accepted) |
| Foreign treatment | Pre-approval required; reimbursement to principal member if invoice is foreign |
| Dual insurance | Member must disclose; Britam pays only its **proportionate share** |
| Third-party injury | Britam may pay then seek recovery (subrogation) |
| Fraudulent claims | Benefits forfeited; member removed from **date of entry**; **no premium refund** |

**Cancellation refund:** If policy cancelled with 30-day notice, unused premium may be refunded **minus claims paid and admin costs**.

## 6. Quick comparison

| Topic | Britam (contract) | Old Mutual (tariff) |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Products | Corporate **inpatient-only** | Corporate, retail, Heza, Ishema |
| Co-pay | **0%** on referenced schedule | Not specified in tariff (limits + premiums only) |
| Premium timing | Annual, **before** cover | Tariff lists annual premium **ranges** by plan |
| Outpatient | **Excluded** | Priced separately on most plans |
| Limits | Per-family sub-limits in schedule | Explicit RWF ranges per benefit tier |
| Mutuelle levy | **5%** on Britam corporate premium | Not in tariff extract |
